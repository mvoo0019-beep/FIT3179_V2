UV Exposure & Skin Cancer in Australia (2024)

Introduction

UV light exposure in Australia is among the highest in the world due to the country's closeness to the equator and clear skies, leading to the country also having one of the highest skin cancer rates in the world. The donut chart on the right shows melanoma, the most dangerous type of skin cancer having one of the highest estimated incidence rates among all types of cancer in Australia. This dashboard will explore UV exposure around the country, regional UV exposure trends, demographic skin cancer rates, and finally the correlation between UV exposure and skin cancer rates.

Seasonal and Regional UV Exposure

This section explores how ultraviolet (UV) exposure varies across Australian cities. The map highlights average UV levels for each city and month, while the line chart shows how UV intensity changes throughout the year. Northern and western regions consistently have higher UV levels, while the south eastern cities have lower average UV exposure. Overall, the UV exposure drops as winter approaches, then rises again towards summer.

Skin Cancer by Age & Sex

Skin cancer risk rises steeply with age and is higher in females until the 40-44 group, then males consistently have higher rates at higher age groups. Older men are especially vulnerable, likely due to greater outdoor activity and less consistent sunscreen use. These findings highlight the need for gender- and age-specific prevention strategies.

This scatter plot shows the relationship between UV exposure and melanoma incidence rate (per 100k population) in Australian cities. It shows a very slight positive correlation between the two variables across Australia. The states like Queensland, Western Australia and Northern Territories have the highest UV exposures and are also among the highest in melanoma incidence rates. This shows that UV exposure can be a strong cause for skin cancer incidence, and the importance of uv protection.
